# PART 1 ,  THE HEADLINE ENGINE

## 1.1 Principle
A headline SELECTS the right person · PROMISES a result/intrigue · CATCHES in 1 sec. Start from the market's existing DESIRE, never the enemy/product. Colder reader → further from the product.

## 1.2 The matrix (this is what makes hundreds of headlines, not one reworded)
Every headline = **AVATAR × DESIRE (LF8) × AWARENESS × SOPHISTICATION × ANGLE/FORMULA × OUTCOME×EMOTION.** Spread a batch across DESIRES first (different motive), then vary the rest. A batch that reworks ONE idea = reject.

## 1.2a AVATAR is the TOP axis (the multiplier)
The SAME matrix cell, written for a DIFFERENT avatar, becomes a different headline , because each avatar has different lived TRIGGERS and a different belief to flip. One matrix × N avatars = endless headlines. So PICK THE AVATAR FIRST, then fill every cell with THAT avatar's concrete triggers (her mirror, her grandkids, her old photos, her future face at 65). Never a generic line that fits any avatar.
Define an avatar by: WHO she is · WHERE in awareness · her dominant PAINS · the BELIEF that must flip · her lived TRIGGERS (the scenes she recognises).

## 1.2b LANDER → AVATAR → AD (run this whenever given a lander)
1. Read the lander; extract the AVATAR it argues to (who, awareness, pains, current beliefs).
2. Name the PERSPECTIVE-SHIFT the lander makes , its core thesis/mechanism (what it makes her newly believe).
3. Write ad headlines that open the DOOR into that exact thesis: agitate the SAME problem the lander resolves, promise the SAME shift. CONGRUENCY = ad hook ≙ lander's thesis (at problem-level), never a different promise that breaks on click.

## 1.3 Axis ,  LF8 desires (the engine of want, Whitman). The desire is the driver; the product angle is only the vehicle.
1 Survival/vitality · 2 Food/social table · 3 Freedom from fear/pain/risk · 4 Attraction/romance · 5 Comfort/money/convenience · 6 Superiority/status · 7 Care of loved ones · 8 Social approval. (Pick the 4-6 most potent for the niche.)

## 1.4 Axis ,  Awareness (Schwartz; what she can hear)
S5 Unaware (collective truth/moment, don't name the enemy; keep it SIMPLE, not convoluted) · S4 Problem-aware (NAME the problem, agitate the moment) · S3 Solution-aware (contrast the two solution TYPES) · S2 Product-aware (differentiator + proof) · S1 Most-aware (offer/deadline).

## 1.5 Axis ,  Sophistication (Schwartz; how you say it)
1 Direct claim · 2 Amplified claim · 3 Mechanism · 4 Elaborated mechanism · 5 Identification (identity).

## 1.5b Axis , OUTCOME×EMOTION (the punch , the #1 lever)
The punch LANDS on a specific emotional end-state, not a vague "better". LF8 = the broad driver; the OUTCOME = the exact dread/desire the line dramatizes, plus the emotion behind it. Keep a per-brand OUTCOME bank (negative dreads + positive desires, each mapped to a core emotion) and pick the SINGLE strongest outcome for THIS avatar. Wrong/weak outcome = dead line even if every other axis is right.

## 1.6 Hopkins 6-part (run before writing)
Named culprit · consequence (social/emotional) · daily trigger · instant reward · real VOC wording · theme. Principles: named villain > abstraction · hope > pure fear · trigger + reward = the hook.

## 1.7 THE 7 NON-NEGOTIABLES (what makes a headline land)
1. ONE concrete lived MOMENT/observation (a scene she recognises), never an abstract generality.
2. ULTRA-SPECIFIC ,  concrete nouns + real numbers/details. Vague = dead.
3. AIRTIGHT 2-part logic ,  setup + punch that connect, punch LANDS with stakes; the FIRST half must be clear & convincing on its own (no cryptic openers). Never 3 disconnected states.
4. THE RIGHT WORDS ,  say it directly; no vague adjectives; use the precise VOC word.
5. TENSION MATCHES AWARENESS (see 1.4).
6. READS IN ONE PASS , if it must be read twice or decoded, it's out. Plain word order, no tangled clause.
7. THE RIGHT OUTCOME , the punch lands on the STRONGEST emotion for THIS avatar (from the OUTCOME bank, 1.5b), never the first outcome that comes to mind. The outcome must read as a LOSS, never accidentally as a WIN ("look the same" reads as success). Land the emotion through a CONCRETE feared image (a drooping eyelid), not an abstract one ("the face sinks").

## 1.8 Vocabulary discipline
- Forbidden weak/wrong words (per CONFIG) ,  e.g. cold/irrelevant words, ugly adjectives, taxonomy openers („X are two kinds").
- Use the precise problem word, not a pleasant one (the negative must read as negative).
- ROTATE the enemy's synonyms (don't repeat one word every line). ROTATE the facial tell too (not only „forehead"): face · lips · brows · eyelid · cheeks · expression · eyes.
- Use the PRECISE, EVERYDAY word , no invented/poetic verbs (a muscle doesn't „wake up"; it „doesn't recover"). Test: would a 55-year-old say it over coffee.

## 1.9 Voice & mechanics
2nd person, finished thought (setup + punch). No metaphor-as-crutch, no lecture. Max ~18-20 words; long → small setup + big punch. Grammar per language (reflexive placement, articles, proper quotes, NO em dashes, no final period, real numbers only ,  flag unsourced).

## 1.10 CTA ,  optional & selective
NOT on every creative. Add a CTA only when the headline leaves a genuine OPEN LOOP it resolves. A completed thought needs none. The CTA must be SPECIFIC (point at a concrete thing), never vague.

## 1.11 Kill-tests (any NO = rewrite)
1 Gets it in 1 sec without the lander? 2 Concrete picture (drawable)? 3 Two halves connect + landing punch? 4 Every word the right word? 5 Tension matches awareness? 6 Ultra-specific? 7 First half clear & convincing alone? 8 Does EVERY verb literally fit its noun (no metaphor-in-quotes , no „catch a wrinkle"/„the forehead learns")? 9 Does it open a curiosity loop / say something unknown or contra-intuitive, not just state a flat fact?

## 1.12 Spread & dedup at the IDEA level
Distribute by DESIRE first; rotate FORM (story/question/number/secret/reframe/contrast/lived-moment); max 2 per distinct idea; real spread, no flat wall.

# PART 2 ,  THE STATIC VISUAL ENGINE

## 2.1 The job
Scroll-stopping + the headline catches. The catch comes from the CONCEPT + design, not from a stock face.

## 2.2 HARD DESIGN RULES
- **Headline dominates the frame , min 30% (usually 35-40%), NEVER smaller**, two-tier size, one accented word.
- **Full-bleed + integrated** ,  image fills the frame, headline overlaid ON it on a STRONG dark gradient scrim that fades into the image (white/knockout bold). The text NEVER merges with the background , ALWAYS strong contrast so the headline POPS, and ROTATE the contrast device across a batch (dark gradient · clean band · solid colour block · knockout · outline/shadow). A full-bleed band is fine as ONE rotated device; avoid only a shrunk photo boxed in white (the cheap „PowerPoint/Indian-presentation" split).
- **Congruence** ,  the visual literally delivers the headline's claim AND its emotion AND its payoff (if the headline implies she looks bad at 65, SHOW her bad at 65). Wrong emotion (a happy face on a fear line) = fail.
- **Execution, not just idea** ,  a true concept rendered UGLY still fails. Must be polished & appealing.
- **Show the problem + close the ambiguity** , make the angle's SUBJECT unmistakable, whatever it is. If the headline doesn't pin the subject (botox/filler/menopause/senescence/era…), the VISUAL MUST seal it with a cue that fits THAT angle (see 2.4a). Headline + visual = ONE unit; the visual seals what the headline leaves open.
- **VARY EVERYTHING** across a batch (Axis-5 is the #1 sameness sin): visual-type · style/„раса" · composition/shot · concept-type · text-treatment & placement · integration device. No two adjacent share a family.
- People (if shown): per CONFIG ethnicity, coloured (not grey) hair, 50+, INSIDE an interesting concept ,  never a posed stock portrait.
- Text: language-only, perfectly spelled, ZERO duplicated text / quotation marks added / Latin leakage / final period.

## 2.3 FORBIDDEN (never generate)
plain STOCK PORTRAIT + text band (dumbest format) · morbid/gross renders (death-mask & tombstone busts, melting-wax busts, raw-meat/muscle macro, skulls, blank creepy masks) · cowboy/wanted-poster · aged/sepia/yellow paper · native/UGC candid · claymation/kawaii/childish · isometric mini-diorama w/ tiny text · „happy-scene with no product link" (e.g. happy grandma, no botox tell) · mid-century happy-woman · abstract disconnected symbols (slot machine, keys, scales, central clock) · product/bottle „premium commerce" shots (use a real product template only when product is wanted) · text-only-boring · the red-tag+gradient on EVERY creative (it's ONE option, rotate).

## 2.4 VISUAL CONCEPT LIBRARY ,  110 concepts, 10 families (the X-axis)
Full list in the bundled `ETERNA-VISUAL-CONCEPT-LIBRARY.md`. Families:
A Editorial/report (НОВ ДОКЛАД, magazine cover, news-clipping…) · B Infographic/diagram (anatomy callout + red-X, skin cross-section, timeline, cost graph, comparison table…) · C Notebook/hand-drawn explainer (clean paper) · D Pop-art/comic/halftone · E Typographic/text-as-hero (+ small motif) · F Conceptual illustration (split-face, torn portrait, surreal mirror/mask, paper-cut, woodcut, charcoal, gouache, line-art…) · G Photo-conceptual (a person INSIDE a scene: before/after diptych, two-women, dinner, reunion, mirror moment, clinic chair, grandma+child…) · H Clean 3D/render (split face, skin macro, 3D type ,  never morbid) · I Object/scene metaphor (receipts-as-car, treadmill cycle, one-way road, dam/flood, two-paths…) · J Authority/proof (expert quote, reviews graphic, study chart, before/after grid).

## 2.4a THE VISUAL MATRIX (the visual twin of the headline matrix , this is what makes EVERY creative look different by construction)
Every visual = **MEDIUM/FAMILY × STYLE/TECHNIQUE × COMPOSITION/SHOT × PALETTE/MOOD × TEXT-CONTRAST DEVICE × SUBJECT-CUE (per angle).**
- MEDIUM/FAMILY (BIG library): report · diagram/infographic · notebook · pop-art/comic · typographic · conceptual illustration · 3D/render · object-metaphor · authority/proof · museum/artifact (fresco, papyrus, tablet , for unaware) · fake document/form (receipt, label, prescription) · game/cards/quiz · science/lab (microscope, scan, x-ray) · botanical/herbarium · vintage/propaganda poster · sticker/stamp · collage/zine · confession/apology editorial · authentic scrapbook (taped real photos + handwriting) · macro-reveal w/ magnifier · ingredient/herb hero on dark · illustrated before/after portrait · minimalist crossing-lines graph · thermal/heat-map scan · molecular/chemistry structure · vintage metal/enamel sign.
- STYLE/TECHNIQUE (BIG library): woodcut/lino · charcoal/graphite · gouache/watercolour · oil/impasto · flat vector · isometric · halftone/Ben-Day · risograph/screenprint · engraving/etching · ink-wash/sumi-e · pen-and-ink · 3D/CGI · paper-craft/cut-paper · clean claymation · Bauhaus/Memphis/Swiss · art-deco · ancient fresco/mosaic/hieroglyph/illuminated · propaganda poster · blueprint · vintage science plate · neon · low-poly · chalk/pastel · embroidery/textile · photoreal (ONLY as pattern-interrupt). Different handwriting each time.
- COMPOSITION: close-up · wide · split/diptych · object-centered · diagram · grid · profile · top-down · one-subject vs many.
- PALETTE/MOOD: warm editorial · dark cinematic · cream vintage · clean clinical white · punchy pop · teal-charcoal · oxblood · pastel (+ accent colour).
- TEXT-CONTRAST (KEY axis, BIG library): dark gradient · colour block/panel · clean band · knockout · outline/stroke · long shadow · speech-bubble/caption box · marker-highlight behind word · hand-drawn circle/underline · sticker/stamp/tag · torn-paper strip · banner ribbon · embossed · text-as-hero-graphic · duotone block · big-number+tiny-support × position (top/bottom/side) × accent device. Hard: contrast + pops + 30-40%.
- SUBJECT/CONTEXT CUE (per angle, NOT hardcoded): the small element that makes the angle's SUBJECT unmistakable, adapting to the angle , botox→needle/crossed-syringe; fillers→filler/lips; menopause→age/hormones; senescence→zombie/old cells; Cleopatra/unaware→ancient-era motif; cream-mechanism→drop/skin-layer. Be water: the cue follows the angle.
SELECT, don't randomise: (1) CONGRUENCE first , the visual delivers the headline's OUTCOME×EMOTION; (2) then MAX variation , change ≥3 axes per new creative, no two adjacent share Family OR Style, log used (Medium×Style×Palette) triples, never repeat. Combinatorics ≈ families × ~12 styles × ~9 comps × ~8 palettes × ~6 text-devices × ~7 cues = effectively endless.
**PHOTOS OFF BY DEFAULT:** plain photo+headline blends into the feed and gets skipped. Default = NON-photo (illustration/graphic/3D/diagram/artifact , inherently pattern-interrupting). Use a photo ONLY as a deliberate pattern-interrupt (scandalous/uncanny/unique-conceptual), never a plain photo + caption band.
**DENSITY axis (rotate it too):** vary the visual density , from ULTRA-minimalist (white bg, one crossing-lines graph + product, Froya-style) to RICH/dense (macro + magnifier + bold knockout, layered scene). Don't make every creative the same density.

## 2.5 THE MIX ENGINE (hundreds of thousands of unique creatives)
ONE creative = VISUAL CONCEPT (1 of 110) × HEADLINE CELL (LF8 × awareness × sophistication × angle) × INTEGRATION DEVICE (tag/none · gradient/band/clean · top/bottom/side · accent: size/colour/circle/underline/knockout) × PALETTE.
≈ 110 × 200 headline cells × 6 integrations ≈ 100k+, before crop/palette → endless.
MIX moves: cross-pollinate concepts (notebook×anatomy, pop-art×report, charcoal×diptych); same headline × 3 different concepts (test which world wins); same concept × 3 different desires.
Keep a USED-LOG of every (concept × headline) pair; never repeat; rotate families so no two adjacent share a family.

## 2.6 PROMPT TEMPLATE (per creative, encode the design)
`[CONCEPT/style] (NO product unless wanted; show the [problem/needle/вкочанено] where it fits; person = [CONFIG ethnicity], coloured hair, inside the concept, not a posed portrait). Full-bleed, integrated. Bold dominant [language] headline 30-40% on a STRONG contrast device (never merging with the background) reading exactly: [HEADLINE]. [accent word] emphasised by [device]. [subject-cue per angle]. [CTA only if open-loop]. Headline ONCE only, no quotation marks, perfectly spelled, no Latin, no watermark, no duplicated text. Vertical 4:5.`

## 2.7 PRODUCTION (the pipeline that worked)
- Generate via the web tool in a logged-in browser (Chrome) using a paste-automation, to use the unlimited web mode ,  NOT per-call API/MCP (MCP = single cases). Model = Nano Banana 2 @ 1K, 4:5, ~3s pacing with retry-when-Generate-disabled.
- The model selector may default wrong ,  force it back to the cheaper model before each run; verify in the log.
- After generating: review EVERY render (download + look). NB2 sometimes duplicates the headline or adds quotes despite the prompt → cull & regen the defective (~15-25% of a bulk run). A bulk run is NEVER 100% clean.
- ⛔ NEVER delete/remove creations or references in the web UI via automation (broad „remove" selectors can wipe the gallery). To run without a product, simply don't load a product template.

## 2.8 END-TO-END WORKFLOW (cold creation)
1 Fill CONFIG. 2 Diagnose awareness/sophistication. 3 Write headlines across the matrix (Part 1), run kill-tests. 4 Pick a SPREAD of visual concepts (different families × different desires × different integrations) from the library. 5 Build prompts (2.6). 6 Generate (2.7). 7 QA every render, cull/regen defects. 8 Ship the clean spread; log used pairs.

# PART 3 ,  THE VARIATION ENGINE (varying a PROVEN winner, not cold creation)
Use this PART when the task is **„направи вариация / вариации на този уинър / multiply this winner / вариирай"** , i.e. there is already a winning ad and you want MORE of that win. This is different from cold creation (Parts 1-2): a winner already told you what the market wants; do NOT re-guess it.

## 3.0 THE ONE LAW
**Every winner won because of ONE winner element. First DIAGNOSE it. LOCK it. INTERPOLATE concepts around it. Never move the core.** This is universal , every brand, every winner has one. The skill is knowing what NOT to touch.

## 3.1 STEP 1 , DIAGNOSE THE WINNER ELEMENT (the hardest step; ~60% of the quality , never skip)
Find the ONE element doing the work. It can be:
- the **VISUAL** , the main idea/image (a before/after of a body zone, a specific scene/metaphor). **MOST OFTEN it is the visual.**
- the **HEADLINE** , a specific phrase/paradox/moment.
- a specific **PROOF** , a number, a testimonial, a before/after.
**THE TEST , remove it mentally: does the win die?** Whatever kills the ad when removed = the winner element. (Example: remove the before/after of the NECK → it's just another product ad → the win is gone → the neck before/after IS the winner element.)
**PRESENT ≠ CONVERTING.** Never assume the thing that is merely THERE is the trigger. The product/bottle is usually a SUPPORTING actor, never the focus , do not let it become the hero. In [neck + before/after + serum], the serum is present but the neck before/after converts.

## 3.2 STEP 2 , LOCK IT
The winner element appears **recognisably intact and dominant in EVERY variation.** It is the nucleus. Everything else is replaceable wrapping.

## 3.3 STEP 3 , INTERPOLATE AROUND IT (move everything EXCEPT the core; ONE dimension per variation)
- **If the winner element is VISUAL** → lock the visual nucleus; vary the WRAPPING: format (mem „какво поръчах/получих" · testimonial card · product-hero · close-up · split · authority/proof) + headline + layout + which proof element is shown + hero placement. The nucleus (e.g. the neck before/after) stays untouched in the centre; only the wrapper changes.
- **If the winner element is the HEADLINE** → lock the headline DNA; vary the visual archetype/scene (run the Part 2 visual matrix on the same headline).
- Move **exactly ONE** wrapping dimension per variation = attributable learning. **Close enough** = obviously the same campaign; **different enough** = a new Meta Andromeda Entity ID.

## 3.4 STEP 4 , TWO GUARDS (kill the weak ones)
- **DISTANCE:** label each [clone / just-right / drift]. CLONE = only a synonym/tiny swap → wastes a slot. DRIFT = the winner element itself changed → it is NOT a variation, it's a new cold bet → route to cold creation (Parts 1-2), test as new. Only **just-right** ships (nucleus intact, exactly one wrapper moved).
- **WIN-SURVIVES:** remove-test each variation , is the winner element still intact AND dominant? If it got demoted, muddied, or the product/wrapper became the hero → KILL it.

## 3.5 WORKED EXAMPLE (Eterna , the locked standard)
WINNER: a static with the BEFORE/AFTER of the NECK (crepey, ringed neck → firm, smooth neck), product + proof around it.
- Winner element = **the neck before/after transformation (VISUAL).** Why it converts: the neck is where age shows first and most visibly for women 50+, it cannot be hidden, botox does not fix it → instant „this is me" + irrefutable, specific proof.
- The serum is PRESENT but NOT the trigger (supporting actor).
- Variations (nucleus = neck before/after, untouched; wrapper interpolated): „какво поръчах / какво получих" mem · testimonial card („Наистина действа", 5-star, 5000+) · product-hero shot with the neck before/after in a circle · macro close-up of the neck · vertical before/after split. The neck before/after stays dominant in every one.

## 3.6 VARIATION WORKFLOW
1 Identify the winner (its image + any headline/FB copy). 2 DIAGNOSE the winner element via the remove-test (3.1) , state it in one sentence; if you can't name it, you don't understand the win yet → STOP. 3 LOCK it. 4 Write N variations, each holding the nucleus and moving exactly ONE wrapper dimension (3.3); tag the dimension. 5 Run the two guards (3.4); drop clones/drifts. 6 Build prompts that keep the nucleus identical and only re-dress the wrapper. 7 Generate (2.7) via unlimited web mode. 8 QA every render, cull/regen defects. Ship only just-right, win-surviving variations.
